Why cybersecurity matters — to everyone.

// The digital and physical worlds are now the same system.

Your salary, your medical records, the traffic lights on your street, the grid powering your home, the satellites guiding your flights — all run on software, all reachable over networks. A weakness in one node can cascade into hospitals turning patients away, factories halting, and democracies destabilising.

Cybersecurity is the discipline of keeping that system trustworthy. It protects three foundational properties — confidentiality, integrity, and availability — known as the CIA triad. When any of them fail, real people lose money, privacy, safety, or life.

Awareness is the cheapest, most effective control we have. 82% of breaches involve a human element. A trained employee, an informed citizen, a curious kid asking "is this link real?" — each one is a sensor in a planetary defense network.

[ THE CIA TRIAD ]
  • C — Confidentiality
    Only authorised eyes can see the data.
  • I — Integrity
    Data is unaltered and trustworthy.
  • A — Availability
    Systems are up when they're needed.
